Simple Crockpot Carnitas

Description:

Carnitas is a dish of pork traditionally braised in its own fat for several hours, then fried at the end to make it deliciously crispy.

It is best served (in my opinion) on lightly fried corn tortillas with cilantro and onions.

Ingredients:

  • 7-9lb pork shoulder
  • 1 large white onion
  • 8 cloves of garlic
  • 2 large orange juiced
  • 3 limes juiced
  • 1 small bottle or can of coke (preferably mexican coke)
  • 2 tbsp mexican oregano
  • 2 tbsp ground cumin
  • 2 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • corn tortillas
  • cilanro and white onion for garnish

Steps:

  1. Combine everything in crock pot making sure to distribute the spices evenly.
  2. Cook in crock pot for 8 hours on low.
  3. Once cooked, take meat and shred (it should fall apart) and place on a baking sheet with 1 cup of reserved liquid.
  4. Place baking sheet in oven on broil (high) for 15 minutes, turning the pan as needed. Pay attention to it when it is in the broiler as to not burn it.
  5. Lightly fry corn tortillas in some veggie oil and serve with cilantro, onions, and your choice of salsa.
